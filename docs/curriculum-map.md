# Curriculum Map — English Adventure 2

## Course profile

- Learner: nine-year-old primary ESL learner.
- Main interface language: English.
- Optional support language: Persian with correct RTL display.
- Delivery: static website suitable for GitHub Pages.
- Progression: Welcome Unit, Units 1–9, three checkpoints, and original Starters-style practice.

## Standard main-unit sequence

Each main unit follows this pedagogical sequence:

1. Vocabulary introduction
2. Song-based or rhythmic language exposure — create a new original chant rather than copying the book song
3. Illustrated story — create a new original story with the same language targets
4. Language in Action dialogue
5. Grammar discovery and practice
6. Content Connection / CLIL
7. Culture Connection
8. Phonics
9. Values and project
10. Vocabulary and grammar review
11. Speaking review

## Detailed learning progression

### Welcome Unit

Build automatic recognition of classroom items, family words, daily actions, and basic location language. Review singular/plural location questions, present-continuous questions, and `Does ... have...?`.

### Unit 1

Move from naming classroom activities to describing individuals and groups using the present continuous. Introduce quantities through `there is/there are` and `How many...?`. Extend language through arithmetic and numbers to 100.

### Unit 2

Move from naming outdoor activities to stating personal and third-person preferences. Add spatial relations and apply them to playground scenes. Connect movement to bones and muscles and introduce safe-play decisions.

### Unit 3

Organise household vocabulary by rooms. Practise singular/plural object-location answers and ownership using possessive `'s`. Compare old and modern objects and explore household solutions from different cultures.

### Unit 4

Connect places with purposes using `want/wants to`. Ask whether a place exists and locate it on a map. Compare transport methods and practise a procedural street-safety sequence.

### Unit 5

Name jobs, ask about ambitions, and connect ambitions with interests and reasons. Distinguish goods and services. Encourage goal-setting without implying that a child must choose one career permanently.

### Unit 6

Read and set o'clock times, ask `when` questions, and describe routines in the present simple. Explore historical timekeeping and work schedules. Practise punctuality planning.

### Unit 7

Ask and answer about food preferences in singular and plural forms. Use positive and negative preference statements. Read simple nutritional information and make balanced, non-shaming food choices.

### Unit 8

Name animals and ask about abilities with `can/can't`. Match animals to habitats and discuss ways humans help animals. Use appreciative adjectives and avoid presenting wild animals as pets.

### Unit 9

Order and spell months, connect months to seasons and events, and use `always/never` with present-simple routines. Compare celebrations respectfully and build a personal annual calendar.

## Checkpoint design

Each checkpoint should include:

- learner confidence check before assessment;
- integrated listening and gap-fill;
- picture/map/scene-based speaking;
- mixed vocabulary and grammar tasks;
- one personal but privacy-safe writing or drawing task;
- mastery summary and recommended review;
- confidence check after assessment.

## Starters-style practice

Create entirely new assets and questions using these task families:

- listening: choose one of three pictures;
- listening: write a name or number from a scene;
- reading and writing: check/cross picture statements;
- reading and writing: unscramble a pictured word;
- reading and writing: complete a short text from a word box;
- speaking: describe a large scene and answer short prompts.
