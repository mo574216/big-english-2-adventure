# Content, Copyright, Privacy, and Pedagogy Guidelines

## Mandatory copyright boundary

The source book is a curriculum reference only.

Do not commit, publish, extract, or recreate:

- the source PDF;
- scanned textbook pages;
- the cover or publisher branding;
- publisher photographs, illustrations, characters, stickers, or cutouts;
- original audio recordings;
- song lyrics or melodies;
- full stories, long dialogues, or substantial exercise text;
- page layouts that imitate the source book.

Allowed curriculum facts include:

- unit order and titles;
- vocabulary items;
- grammar targets and short example patterns;
- phonics graphemes and example words;
- CLIL, culture, values, and learning outcomes;
- source page references stored as internal metadata.

Create new examples, new dialogues, new stories, new listening scripts, new SVG scenes, and new questions.

Display this footer in the application:

> Unofficial personal practice companion. Not affiliated with or endorsed by the original publisher.

## Originality requirements

Every reading text, dialogue, listening script, chant, scene, and assessment item must be newly written. Reusing a target phrase such as `What does she want to be?` is acceptable because it is the grammar target. Reproducing a complete source dialogue or story is not.

Avoid using the original recurring textbook characters. Create a new cast and a clearly distinct visual identity.

## Child safety and privacy

- No account, email, full name, birth date, or address is required.
- Do not request school name, exact location, or photographs.
- Store progress locally in the browser.
- Provide JSON export/import controlled by the parent.
- Do not use advertising, analytics trackers, social sharing, public profiles, or rankings.
- Speaking recordings remain local and are deleted when the learner chooses.
- Browser speech recognition is optional and must never be described as a precise pronunciation score.

## Language policy

- Learner UI is English-first.
- Persian help is optional and concise.
- Persian blocks use `dir="rtl"` and an appropriate readable font stack.
- English target language remains visible during practice.
- Persian support should explain meaning or instructions, not replace the English activity.

## Pedagogical policy

- Sessions should fit approximately 10–20 minutes.
- Use one primary task per screen.
- Give immediate, specific, non-punitive feedback.
- Permit a second attempt before revealing an answer.
- Recycle vocabulary across later units.
- Mix recognition, controlled production, and freer production.
- Do not label foods as morally “good” or “bad”; use balanced language such as everyday and sometimes foods.
- Present cultures respectfully and avoid stereotypes.
- Present careers as possibilities rather than fixed expectations.

## Feedback language

Recommended:

- Great work!
- Almost — check the last word.
- Listen one more time.
- Try a different answer.
- You improved.
- New word learned.

Avoid red failure screens, ridicule, loss of earned rewards, and time pressure for core learning tasks.

## Visual assets

Use original SVG/CSS illustrations or clearly licensed generic icons. Keep a source record for any third-party icon. Do not scrape images from search engines.
